Profit and Pricing Strategy Calculator

Definitions:

  • Sales - Number of sales for a product. This will be used to calculate totals.
  • Product Cost - Cost to make a product. This will be subtracted from price to calculate profit. It will also be added to expenses in totals.
  • Expense Cost - Cost for an expense. This is how much you need to pay for anything not associated with a sale. It will also be added to expenses in totals.
  • Commission - Percent of a sale for a product that an employee would be paid. This is an expense and will be subtracted from the total profit.
  • Net Percentage - Percent of price you make as profit. This input will be used to generate price.
  • Price - Generated sales price for a product. This is an output from commission, cost and net percentage generated automatically
  • Frequency - Recurring(monthly) or One-time(individual). This will determine whether something is used once or added multiple times in the totals.
  • Quantity - Number of expenses. This will be used to calculate totals.

Calculations:

Price = Cost/(1 - Net Percentage - Commission)

Example: Cost = $10, Commission = 10%, Net Percentage = 50%

$10/(1 - 0.5 - 0.1) = $25

Profit Per Sale = Price - Cost - Commission

Example: Cost = $10, Commission = 10%, Price = $25

$25 - $10 - ($25*0.1) = $12.50

Net Profit(Totals) = Revenue - Cost - Commission

Example: Cost = $200, Commission = $200, Revenue = $2000

$2000 - $200 - $200 = $1600

Net Profit Percentage(Totals) = Net Profit/Revenue

Example: Net Profit = $1600, Revenue = $2000

$1600/$2000 = 80%
